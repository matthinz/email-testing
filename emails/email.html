About the United States Department of Ice Cream

The United States Department of Ice Cream was established in the early 1800s in response to the demand for frozen desserts from the burgeoning population. The first ice cream recipes were created by Thomas Jefferson and George Washington in the late 1700s, and the popularity of these recipes quickly spread across the country. The Department of Ice Cream was founded to ensure that these recipes and the quality of the frozen treats remained consistent and safe for consumption. The Department quickly grew in size and scope, eventually adding a research and development division to innovate new flavors and techniques for making and serving ice cream. The Department also worked to ensure the safety of ice cream production, setting standards for cleanliness and hygiene in ice cream factories. Today, the Department of Ice Cream remains a vital part of the US food system, providing oversight and regulation to ensure the quality and safety of ice cream production and consumption.
